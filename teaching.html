Teaching & Education

Graduate and Undergraduate Courses in Computational Linguistics

Courses Offered

The CLLT lab offers a range of courses at the undergraduate and graduate level, focusing on both theoretical foundations and practical applications in computational linguistics and natural language processing.

Introduction to Computational Linguistics

Undergraduate

Foundational course covering basic concepts in computational linguistics, including morphology, syntax, semantics, and practical NLP applications.

Advanced Natural Language Processing

Graduate

Deep dive into state-of-the-art NLP techniques including transformer models, language understanding, generation, and evaluation methods.

Computational Semantics

Graduate

Formal approaches to meaning representation, semantic parsing, inference systems, and neuro-symbolic integration.

Digital Humanities & Corpus Linguistics

Graduate

Computational methods for analyzing historical texts, corpus construction, annotation, and digital philology tools.

Student Supervision

Graduate Research Opportunities

We supervise MA and PhD students in computational linguistics, natural language processing, and related areas. Our students work on cutting-edge research projects and publish in top-tier conferences and journals.

Current Research Topics

  • Greek language technology and dialectal NLP
  • Neuro-symbolic AI systems
  • Digital humanities and ancient text analysis
  • Low-resource language processing
  • Computational semantics and formal linguistics
  • Knowledge graph extraction and reasoning
