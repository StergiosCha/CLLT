Our Approach

CLLT's work on natural language inference (NLI) is distinguished by its integration of formal semantic theory with computational implementation. Unlike pure machine learning approaches that treat NLI as pattern matching, we build systems grounded in type-theoretical semantics and proof theory.

Our research demonstrates that linguistic theory enhances computational systems: our symbolic NLI system achieves 81% accuracy on the FraCaS test suite (best performance by a logical system) and was the first to complete the challenging anaphora and ellipsis sections. This work bridges the gap between neural scalability and symbolic interpretability.

Why This Matters

Explainable AI

Our proof-assistant-based approach produces human-readable logical proofs for inferences, addressing the black-box problem in neural NLI systems. This is crucial for applications requiring transparency and accountability.

Semantic Precision

Type-theoretical semantics provides compositional meaning representations that capture subtle semantic distinctions missed by distributional approaches. Our work on FraCaS demonstrates superior handling of quantifiers, temporal relations, and complex semantic phenomena.

Low-Resource Languages

Symbolic approaches require less training data than pure neural methods, making them ideal for under-resourced languages like Greek dialects. Our Greek NLI resources enable research that would be impossible with data-hungry transformers alone.

Hybrid Future

We advocate for neural-symbolic integration: using neural models for robust parsing and representation learning, combined with symbolic reasoning for precise inference. This synthesis offers both scalability and interpretability.
