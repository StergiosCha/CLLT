About the Lab

The Computational Linguistics and Language Technology Lab (CLLT) is the only NLP lab at the University of Crete, uniquely positioned at the intersection of theoretical linguistics, computer science, and cognitive science.

CLLT specializes in neural-symbolic AI/NLP and type-theoretical semantics, bringing formal linguistic rigor to modern computational approaches. Our research demonstrates that deep theoretical understanding enhances machine learning systems, particularly for under-resourced languages and complex semantic phenomena.

We maintain active research programs in Greek NLP resources, natural language inference with proof assistants, computational dialectology, and digital humanities applications. The lab is led by Professor Stergios Chatzikyriakidis and collaborates extensively with leading institutions including Cambridge, Gothenburg, and London.

Our unique approach: We don't just apply transformers to language‚Äîwe understand language structure through formal linguistics, then build computational systems informed by that understanding. This makes our work particularly valuable for explainable AI, under-resourced languages, and applications requiring semantic precision.

Our Research Philosophy

"We believe that formal linguistic theory and neural computation are complementary, not competing approaches. Type-theoretical semantics provides the precision and interpretability that pure machine learning lacks, while neural models offer the scalability and robustness that symbolic systems need. Our work demonstrates that the synthesis of these approaches yields systems that are both powerful and explainable‚Äîessential for building AI we can trust."
