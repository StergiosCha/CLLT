About the Lab

The Computational Linguistics and Language Technology Lab (CLLT) focuses on promoting research and education in Natural Language Processing on both a practical and theoretical level. The lab fosters interdisciplinary research between Linguistics, Computer Science, and Cognitive Science.

The lab specializes in generative as well as neuro-symbolic AI/NLP and is the only NLP lab at the University of Crete. The lab also has an active interest in emerging new technologies in Digital Humanities.

CLLT is based at the Department of Philology, University of Crete, and is currently headed by Professor Stergios Chatzikyriakidis.

Research Focus

Natural Language Processing

Advanced computational methods for understanding and generating human language

Neuro-Symbolic AI

Combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning for robust language understanding

Greek Language Technology

Specialized resources and tools for Greek and its dialectal varieties

Digital Humanities

Computational approaches to classical texts and historical linguistics
