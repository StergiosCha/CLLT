News & Updates

Latest achievements from CLLT

Publications · Talks · Datasets · Funding · Collaborations

November 2025
Plenary Talk: Professor Chatzikyriakidis delivers plenary lecture at Linguistic Sciences conference in Thessaloniki, Greece, titled "From Ancient Greek to Modern AI: Computational Approaches to Language."
September 2025
Conference Presentations: Two papers accepted - "Reverse Engineering NLI" at COLM 2025 and "On Tables with Numbers" at LM4UC workshop (NAACL).
August 2025
Invited Talk: Professor Chatzikyriakidis invited to deliver plenary talk at international conference in Albania on computational linguistics and AI.
May 2025
Cambridge Presentation: Selected to present at 17th International Conference on Greek Linguistics, University of Cambridge, UK.
2025 (In Press)
Two Cambridge Books: "Theories of Types and the Structure of Meaning" and "Dialogical Interaction, Types, and the Structure of Meaning" accepted for Cambridge Elements in Semantics series. Co-authored with Robin Cooper, Eleni Gregoromichelaki, and Peter Sutton.
2025
Poetry Generation Paper: "Poetry in RAGs" accepted at NLP4DH 2025 (NAACL). Novel RAG-based system for modern Greek interwar poetry generation, collaboration with Anastasia Natsina.
March 2024
Major Publication: OYXOY: A Modern NLP Test Suite for Modern Greek accepted at Findings of ACL: EACL 2024. First multi-label NLI dataset accounting for semantic ambiguity. Full dataset and code released on GitHub and Zenodo.
2024
Special Issue Published: "Natural Logic Meets Machine Learning" special issue published in Journal of Logic, Language and Information, Volume 33(1). Co-edited with Aikaterini-Lida Kalouli and Lasha Abzianidze.
2024
Greek Book on AI: Published "Τεχνητή Νοημοσύνη και Μεγάλα Γλωσσικά Μοντέλα: Ιστορία, Χρήσεις, Ανησυχίες" (Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models) with Diavlos Books, making AI accessible to Greek-speaking audiences.
August 2023
Dataset Release: GRDD (Greek Regional Dialects Dataset) released. First comprehensive dataset for Greek dialectal NLP covering Cypriot, Pontic, Cretan, and Northern Greek varieties. Paper on arXiv.
2023
CSLI Volume Published: "Probabilistic Approaches to Linguistic Theory" edited volume published by CSLI Publications. Co-edited with Jean-Philippe Bernardy, Rasmus Blanck, Shalom Lappin, and Aleksandre Maskharashvili.
2023
NALOMA IV: Fourth edition of Natural Logic Meets Machine Learning workshop organized at IWCS 2023. Proceedings published in ACL Anthology.
2023-Present
Major EU Funding: CLLT joins €2.5M TALOS-AI4SSH ERA Chair project at University of Crete, strengthening AI research capacity and European collaboration networks.
2023
Oxford Book Chapter: "The emergence of prepositional genitives in Northern Greek beyond suppletion" published in "The place of Case in Grammar" (Oxford University Press). Co-authored with Dimitris Michelioudakis and Giorgos Spathas.
2023
Journal Publication: "Propositional Forms of Judgemental Interpretations" published in Journal of Logic, Language and Information, Vol. 32(4). Co-authored with Tianyu Xue and Zhaohui Luo.
2022-Present
ERC Collaboration: Team member of PHYLPROGRAM ERC Advanced Grant project (PI: Elena Anagnostopoulou), investigating linguistic phylogeny and theoretical syntax.
June 2022
LREC 2022: "Fine-grained Entailment: Resources for Greek NLI and Precise Entailment" published at DCLRL workshop, LREC 2022. Released Extended Greek FraCaS (774 examples) and de-dropped Greek XNLI. Code and data on GitHub.
2022
NALOMA III: Third edition of Natural Logic Meets Machine Learning workshop organized at ESSLLI 2022. Proceedings in ACL Anthology.
2022
Model Robustness Study: "How Does Data Corruption Affect Natural Language Understanding Models?" published at *SEM 2022. Systematic investigation of GLUE model robustness. Co-authored with Aarne Talman, Marianna Apidianaki, and Jörg Tiedemann.
2021
FraCaS Breakthrough: "Applied Temporal Analysis: A Complete Run of the FraCaS Test Suite" published at IWCS 2021. Achieved 81% accuracy on FraCaS, best performance by a logical system. First to complete anaphora and ellipsis sections. Co-authored with Jean-Philippe Bernardy.
2019
Wide-Coverage NLI System: "A Wide-Coverage Symbolic Natural Language Inference System" published at NoDaLiDa 2019. First system to complete FraCaS anaphora/ellipsis sections using type-theoretical semantics.
