Research

Our Current Projects and Research Areas

Research Areas

Natural Language Processing

We develop state-of-the-art computational methods for analyzing, understanding, and generating human language. Our work spans both neural and symbolic approaches to NLP, with particular focus on low-resource languages and dialectal varieties.

Neuro-Symbolic AI

Our lab pioneers the integration of neural networks with symbolic reasoning systems. This hybrid approach combines the learning capabilities of deep learning with the interpretability and logical consistency of symbolic AI, enabling more robust and explainable language understanding systems.

Greek Language Technology

We specialize in developing NLP resources and tools for Greek and its dialectal varieties, including Cypriot, Pontic, Cretan, and Northern Greek dialects. Our work includes corpus development, benchmark creation, and model fine-tuning for Greek language varieties.

Digital Humanities

We apply computational methods to classical philology and historical linguistics, particularly focusing on ancient Greek texts. Our work includes knowledge graph extraction, automated analysis of historical texts, and tools for digital scholarship.

Current Projects

MEDEA-NEUMOUSA Platform

Advanced platform for ancient text analysis combining knowledge graph extraction with neuro-symbolic reasoning capabilities. Enables sophisticated computational analysis of classical Greek literature with automated entity recognition and relationship extraction.

Access Platform →

Greek Regional Dialects Dataset (GRDD+)

Comprehensive dataset and benchmark for Greek dialectal varieties with fine-tuning experiments across multiple model architectures. Covers Cypriot, Pontic, Cretan, and Northern Greek dialects.

Paper | Dataset

Linguistic Distance Framework

Seven-dimensional framework for analyzing linguistic change across Indo-European languages, combining computational methods with theoretical linguistics.

Access Platform →

Thucydides goes RAGging (RAG-to-Coq)

Retrieval-augmented generation pipeline that ingests Thucydides' History, builds verified knowledge graphs, and feeds structured claims to Coq for proof obligations. RAG-to-Coq links long-context reading with symbolic verification, enabling explainable answers about classical events and actors.

Prototype combines semantic chunking, contrastive retrieval, and proof-term synthesis.
